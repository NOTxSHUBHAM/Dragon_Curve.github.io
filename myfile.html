About the Dragon Curve

The Dragon Curve is a fractal that can be created by repeatedly folding a strip of paper in half. It was first described by mathematician John Heighway in the 1960s.

Despite its simple construction rules, the Dragon Curve exhibits complex self-similar patterns and has applications in computer graphics, antenna design, and mathematics education.

This enhanced version fixes rendering issues, adds new visualization options, and improves performance.
